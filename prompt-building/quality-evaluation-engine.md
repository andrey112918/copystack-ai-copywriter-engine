# Quality Evaluation Engine

## Purpose

The Quality Evaluation Engine is responsible for evaluating every generated output before it is returned to the user.

Its purpose is not to rewrite copy.

Its purpose is to determine whether the copy meets CopyStack's quality standards.

If it does not, it must trigger revision before delivery.

No copy should bypass this evaluation.

---

# Core Philosophy

Good copy is not judged by how impressive it sounds.

Good copy is judged by how effectively it helps the intended audience take the desired action.

Quality is measured by usefulness, clarity and strategic alignment.

---

# Evaluation Process

Every piece of copy must be evaluated against multiple dimensions.

Strategic

↓

Structural

↓

Psychological

↓

Linguistic

↓

Ethical

↓

Technical

Only after passing every dimension should the copy be approved.

---

# Overall Scoring

Each category receives a score from 1–10.

A score below the minimum threshold triggers revision.

Suggested thresholds:

9–10 = Excellent

8 = Strong

7 = Acceptable

6 = Needs revision

5 or below = Reject and rewrite

---

# Category 1 — Objective Alignment

Question:

Does the copy clearly support the intended business objective?

Evaluate:

Primary goal

CTA alignment

Message focus

Conversion intent

One clear objective

---

# Category 2 — Audience Alignment

Question:

Does the copy sound like it was written specifically for this audience?

Evaluate:

Relevant vocabulary

Appropriate tone

Pain points

Desired outcomes

Customer awareness

Objections

Emotional relevance

---

# Category 3 — Positioning

Evaluate:

Unique mechanism

Differentiation

Value communication

Competitive distinction

Transformation clarity

If competitors could easily replace their name with yours, positioning is weak.

---

# Category 4 — Clarity

Evaluate:

Sentence simplicity

Logical progression

Readability

Information hierarchy

Ease of scanning

Absence of ambiguity

Readers should never have to reread a sentence.

---

# Category 5 — Persuasion

Evaluate:

Problem definition

Emotional movement

Trust

Proof

Benefits

Objection handling

Momentum

CTA strength

Persuasion should feel natural—not forced.

---

# Category 6 — Credibility

Supported claims

Evidence

Transparency

Realistic promises

Specificity

Trust signals

---

# Category 7 — Emotional Impact

Primary emotion

Emotional consistency

Desired future

Pain acknowledgement

Transformation

The emotional journey should feel coherent.

---

# Category 8 — Flow

Evaluate:

Transitions

Paragraph order

Narrative momentum

Section progression

Rhythm

No paragraph should feel isolated.

---

# Category 9 — Natural Language

Evaluate:

Human tone

Sentence variation

Conversational flow

Natural vocabulary

Absence of AI clichés

The copy should feel written—not generated.

# Category 10 — Platform Fit

Length

Formatting

CTA style

Attention span

Platform conventions

Landing Page

Meta Ads

LinkedIn

Google Ads

Email

TikTok

SEO

# Category 11 — Technical Accuracy

Verify:

Names

Links

Numbers

Pricing

Dates

Claims

Terminology

---

# Category 12 — Ethical Compliance

Confirm:

No fabricated testimonials

No false urgency

No misleading guarantees

No manipulation

No plagiarism

No unsupported claims

---

# Critical Failure Conditions

Immediately reject output if:

Business objective is unclear.

Audience is inconsistent.

CTA is missing.

Claims are fabricated.

Positioning contradicts itself.

Tone changes dramatically.

Important objections remain unanswered.

The copy sounds obviously AI-generated.

---

# Strength Detection

Identify the strongest parts.

Examples:

Excellent hook.

Strong CTA.

Clear positioning.

Powerful emotional flow.

Natural storytelling.

Effective proof.

Knowing what works helps preserve quality during revision.

---

# Weakness Detection

Identify:

Generic wording

Weak benefits

Repetition

Unclear transitions

Low specificity

Weak proof

Passive voice

Unnecessary complexity

Weak differentiation

Each weakness should receive a recommendation.

---

# Revision Priority

Fix problems in this order:

Objective

↓

Audience

↓

Positioning

↓

Offer

↓

Structure

↓

Persuasion

↓

Language

↓

Grammar

Never polish wording before fixing strategy.

---

# Internal Report

The engine should internally generate:

Overall Score

Category Scores

Strengths

Weaknesses

Priority Revisions

Approval Status

This report is normally hidden from the user unless requested.

---

# Approval Logic

Approve only if:

No critical failures.

Average score ≥ 8.5.

No category below 8.

CTA is clear.

Claims are credible.

Positioning is consistent.

Language sounds human.

Otherwise revise automatically.

---

# Continuous Learning

Repeated weaknesses should become improvement signals for future generations.

The goal is consistent improvement—not perfect first drafts.

---

# Final Rule

Copy should never be delivered simply because it is complete.

It should be delivered only when it is strategically sound, psychologically persuasive and genuinely useful.

Quality is not the final step.

Quality is the standard every step must achieve.
