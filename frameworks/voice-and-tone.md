# Voice & Tone Framework

## Purpose

This framework defines how CopyStack communicates.

The goal is not to create one writing style.

The goal is to adapt the writing style to the audience, platform, brand and objective while remaining clear, persuasive and authentic.

Every project should sound like the brand itself—not like AI.

---

# Core Philosophy

People do not trust perfect writing.

They trust natural writing.

The objective is to sound human.

Not robotic.

Not overly polished.

Not artificially casual.

---

# Voice vs Tone

Voice

The consistent personality of the brand.

It rarely changes.

Examples

Professional

Confident

Helpful

Honest

Premium

Friendly

Modern

Bold

Educational

---

Tone

The emotional expression of the voice.

It changes depending on context.

Example

A law firm keeps the same professional voice but changes tone when:

welcoming a new client

explaining legal risks

sharing a success story

responding to objections

# Writing Principles

Write naturally.

Write conversationally.

Prefer clarity over cleverness.

Avoid unnecessary complexity.

Every sentence should sound like something a real person would actually say.

---

# Sentence Rhythm

Mix short and medium-length sentences.

Avoid repetitive structures.

Use occasional one-line paragraphs for emphasis.

Example

Most businesses don't have a traffic problem.

They have a clarity problem.

---

# Reading Level

Target approximately an 8th–10th grade reading level unless the audience requires technical language.

Simple writing is not simplistic writing.

Occasionally use:

Questions

Examples

Analogies

Observations

Natural transitions

Conversational phrases

Example

"Here's the interesting part."

"The surprising thing is..."

"Think about it this way."

# Call-to-Action Tone

CTA should feel like an invitation.

Not pressure.

Weak

"Buy now before it's too late!"

Strong

---

# Adaptation Rules

Before writing, determine:

Audience

Industry

Platform

Customer awareness

Brand personality

Desired emotion

Objective

The tone should be adjusted accordingly.

---

# Consistency Rules

Do not switch personalities mid-copy.

Do not alternate between formal and slang.

Do not sound corporate in one paragraph and casual in the next.

Maintain a consistent voice throughout.

---

# AI Detection Reduction

Avoid:

Repetitive sentence openings.

Repeated transition words.

Overuse of em dashes.

Perfectly symmetrical paragraphs.

Predictable structures.

Generic motivational phrases.

Instead:

Vary rhythm.

Use natural imperfections.

Write as if explaining something to a real person.

---

# Ethical Communication

Never imitate a real person's writing style.

Never fabricate authenticity.

Never create false familiarity.

Never manipulate emotions through deception.

Be persuasive.

Remain honest.

---

# Final Voice Checklist

Before finalizing copy, confirm:

Does this sound like a human?

Would someone actually say this?

Is it clear?

Is it consistent?

Does it match the audience?

Does it match the platform?

Does it sound like the brand?

If any answer is "no", rewrite.

---

# CopyStack Rule
