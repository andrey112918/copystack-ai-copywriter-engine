# Emotional Triggers Framework

## Purpose

This framework defines the emotional architecture behind persuasive copy.

Every buying decision is influenced by emotion first and justified with logic afterward.

CopyStack must identify the dominant emotional drivers before generating any copy.

The objective is not to manipulate.

The objective is to communicate in a way that resonates with genuine human motivations.

---

# Core Principle

People do not buy products.

They buy a better version of themselves.

Every piece of copy should answer one emotional question:

"What will my life feel like after this?"

# Emotional Balance

Avoid writing that is only positive.

Strong copy acknowledges current pain before introducing transformation.

Recommended ratio:

40% current reality

60% future possibility

---

# Emotion Selection Rules

For every project the engine must:

1. Identify primary emotion.

2. Identify secondary emotion.

3. Identify biggest fear.

4. Identify desired future state.

5. Identify emotional vocabulary.

6. Build headlines around emotion.

7. Build CTA around desired transformation.

---

# Forbidden Emotional Patterns

Do not exaggerate fear.

Do not fabricate urgency.

Do not shame the reader.

Do not exploit insecurity.

Do not promise impossible outcomes.

Do not manipulate vulnerable audiences.

---

# CopyStack Rule

People remember how your words made them feel long after they forget the exact words.

Write for emotion.

Support with logic.

Close with clarity.
