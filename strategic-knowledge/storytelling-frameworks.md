# Storytelling Framework Library

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

This framework defines when, why and how CopyStack should use storytelling to improve persuasion.

Stories are not decoration.

Stories are strategic tools that increase understanding, memorability and emotional engagement.

The engine should never add a story simply because stories are considered effective.

Every story must move the reader closer to the intended action.

---

# Core Philosophy

Facts explain.

Stories demonstrate.

People rarely remember information.

They remember experiences.

A well-constructed story allows the reader to experience the transformation before making a decision.

---

---

# When NOT To Use Storytelling

Avoid long stories when:

The user searches for an immediate solution.

The platform has limited attention.

The CTA is urgent.

The customer already wants to buy.

Technical clarity is more important.

Examples:

Google Ads

Pricing Pages

Checkout Pages

Support Documentation

Short Meta Ads

Simple Product Pages

---

# When Storytelling Works Best

Use storytelling when:

Trust must be built.

Transformation must be visualized.

Beliefs need to change.

The purchase is emotional.

The offer is complex.

Differentiation is difficult.

Examples:

Landing Pages

VSL

Founder Stories

Case Studies

About Pages

Email Sequences

Long-form Sales Pages

---

# Story Objectives

Stories should accomplish one objective.

Educate.

Build trust.

Reduce objections.

Demonstrate transformation.

Create emotional connection.

Increase memorability.

Never attempt multiple objectives in one story.

---

Real decision.

# Ethical Guidelines

Never fabricate stories.

Never invent customer experiences.

Never exaggerate results.

Never manipulate emotions through false narratives.

Every story should be truthful, relevant and useful.

---

# Final Rule

A story should never exist to entertain.

It should exist to increase understanding, trust and confidence.

If the same objective can be achieved more clearly without a story, choose clarity over storytelling.
