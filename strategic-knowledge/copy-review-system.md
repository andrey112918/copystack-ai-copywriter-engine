# Copy Review System

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

The Copy Review System is the final strategic audit performed before any deliverable is approved.

Its purpose is not to rewrite content.

Its purpose is to verify that the content meets CopyStack's quality standards.

Every asset should pass this review before delivery.

---

# Core Philosophy

Good copy is intentional.

Excellent copy survives rigorous review.

The objective is not to find perfection.

The objective is to eliminate preventable weaknesses.

---

# Review Process

Every deliverable passes through the following reviews.

Strategic Review

↓

Audience Review

↓

Conversion Review

↓

Trust Review

↓

Language Review

↓

Platform Review

↓

Technical Review

↓

Final Approval

Failure in one stage should trigger targeted revision.

---

# Review Scoring

Each category receives:

Excellent (9–10)

Strong (8)

Acceptable (7)

Needs Revision (6)

Reject (1–5)

Minimum approval:

Overall average ≥ 8.5

No category below 8.

---

# Strategic Review

Questions

Is the objective obvious?

Is there one primary goal?

Is positioning consistent?

Is the offer compelling?

Is the transformation clear?

Does every section support the objective?

---

# Audience Review

Questions

Does this speak to one audience?

Does it match awareness?

Does it match the buyer journey?

Are objections addressed?

Does the language feel familiar?

Would the audience recognize themselves?

---

# Offer Review

Questions

Is the offer valuable?

Is the mechanism clear?

Is the value stack logical?

Does price appear after value?

Is risk reduced?

Would the offer still be attractive without discounts?

---

# Trust Review

Questions

Is proof sufficient?

Are claims supported?

Is transparency maintained?

Are guarantees realistic?

Does confidence increase throughout the copy?

Would a skeptical reader trust this?

---

# Conversion Review

Questions

Is there one CTA?

Is friction minimized?

Is momentum maintained?

Does each section naturally lead to the next?

Would the next step feel obvious?

---

# Readability Review

Questions

Are sentences easy to read?

Is scanning easy?

Are paragraphs short enough?

Is the hierarchy clear?

Could any sentence be simplified?

---

# Emotional Review

Questions

What is the primary emotion?

Is it consistent?

Does emotion support trust?

Does the copy create hope without exaggeration?

Is emotional progression logical?

---

# Platform Review

Questions

Does this fit the platform?

Is the structure appropriate?

Is the CTA appropriate?

Is the length appropriate?

Does it follow platform expectations?

---

# Brand Review

Questions

Does this sound like the brand?

Is terminology consistent?

Is positioning consistent?

Is tone consistent?

Could another company publish this unchanged?

---

# Technical Review

Verify

Names

Links

Pricing

Facts

Statistics

Dates

Spelling

Grammar

Formatting

No factual information should be invented.

---

# SEO Review (when applicable)

Questions

Search intent satisfied?

Primary keyword used naturally?

Headings logical?

Meta intent supported?

Readable?

Helpful?

---

# Email Review

Questions

Subject line compelling?

Opening relevant?

Natural flow?

Soft CTA before hard CTA?

Feels personal?

---

# Landing Page Review

Questions

Headline clear?

Value proposition obvious?

Sections logical?

Proof sufficient?

CTA repeated appropriately?

Objections handled?

---

# Advertisement Review

Questions

Attention captured?

Problem relevant?

Offer obvious?

CTA immediate?

Fits attention span?

---

# AI Detection Review

Questions

Does this sound repetitive?

Are there AI clichés?

Is wording overly polished?

Would a human naturally write this?

Is sentence rhythm varied?

Replace anything that feels machine-generated.

---

# Differentiation Review

Questions

Could competitors publish this?

Is the mechanism unique?

Is positioning memorable?

Is specificity high?

---

# Ethical Review

Verify

No fabricated proof.

No fake urgency.

No manipulation.

No hidden conditions.

No misleading promises.

Ethics override conversion.

# Approval Conditions

Approve only when:

Strategic objective is clear.

Audience alignment is strong.

Offer is compelling.

Trust is sufficient.

CTA is appropriate.

Language feels human.

Platform fit is high.

No ethical violations exist.

---

# Automatic Revision Triggers

Automatically revise when:

Headline is weak.

Offer lacks differentiation.

Proof is insufficient.

CTA is unclear.

Readability is poor.

Trust is weak.

Emotion is inconsistent.

Positioning is generic.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Every review should identify reusable lessons.

Patterns of weakness should become future improvements.

Patterns of success should become reusable standards.

The review system should evolve continuously.

---

# Common Mistakes

Reviewing grammar before strategy.

Ignoring audience mismatch.

Optimizing wording instead of offers.

Adding unnecessary complexity.

Treating every platform identically.

Skipping trust evaluation.

# Final Rule

No piece of copy should be delivered simply because it is complete.

It should be delivered only when it is strategically sound, psychologically persuasive, ethically responsible and aligned with the business objective.

Quality is not inspected at the end.

Quality is engineered into every decision.
