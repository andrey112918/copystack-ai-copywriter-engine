# Buyer Journey Framework

## Purpose

The Buyer Journey Framework helps CopyStack understand where a customer is in the buying process and how messaging should evolve at each stage.

Awareness describes what a customer knows.

Buyer Journey describes what a customer is currently trying to accomplish.

Every piece of copy should move the customer one stage forward.

---

# Core Philosophy

Buying is not an event.

Buying is a sequence of decisions.

Every stage introduces new questions, new emotions and new objections.

The role of copy is to reduce uncertainty until the next decision feels obvious.

---

# Journey Overview

Customers move through six stages.

Attention

↓

Interest

↓

Consideration

↓

Evaluation

↓

Decision

↓

Success

CopyStack should identify the current stage before generating content.

---

# Stage 1 — Attention

## Goal

Capture attention.

Interrupt routine.

Spark curiosity.

---

## Customer Mindset

"I'm busy."

"This isn't a priority."

"I'll ignore this unless it feels relevant."

---

## Customer Needs

Relevance

Curiosity

Recognition

Unexpected insight

---

## Recommended Messaging

Unexpected observations

Bold statistics

Questions

Stories

Contrarian ideas

Pattern interruptions

---

## Avoid

Long explanations

Technical language

Product details

Aggressive selling

---

## Success Metric

The customer continues reading.

---

# Stage 2 — Interest

## Goal

Maintain attention.

Increase curiosity.

Demonstrate understanding.

---

## Customer Mindset

"This sounds relevant."

"Tell me more."

---

## Customer Needs

Recognition

Empathy

Understanding

Possibility

---

## Recommended Messaging

Pain points

Desired outcomes

Examples

Short stories

Educational insights

---

## Avoid

Immediate offers

Complex pricing

Detailed implementation

---

## Success Metric

---

# Stage 3 — Consideration

## Goal

---

## Customer Mindset

"I know I need something."

"I'm comparing approaches."

---

## Customer Needs

Trust

Differentiation

Methodology

Expected outcomes

---

## Recommended Messaging

Process

Benefits

Comparison

Proof

---

## Avoid

Generic claims

Exaggeration

Feature dumping

---

## Success Metric

---

# Stage 4 — Evaluation

## Goal

Reduce uncertainty.

Answer objections.

Build confidence.

---

## Customer Mindset

"Can I trust this?"

"Is this right for me?"

---

## Customer Needs

Proof

Testimonials

Case studies

Transparency

Guarantees

Authority

---

## Recommended Messaging

Results

Implementation

FAQs

Pricing explanation

Risk reduction

---

## Avoid

Pressure

Hidden information

Overpromising

---

## Success Metric

---

# Stage 5 — Decision

## Goal

Make action feel easy.

Remove friction.

---

## Customer Mindset

"I'm almost ready."

"I just need confidence."

---

## Customer Needs

Simple process

Expectation setting

---

## Recommended Messaging

Availability

Support

Timeline

Risk reversal

---

## Avoid

Introducing new concepts

Changing positioning

Long educational content

---

## Success Metric

---

# Stage 6 — Success

## Goal

Generate advocacy.

---

## Customer Mindset

---

## Customer Needs

Reassurance

Guidance

Quick wins

Support

Recognition

---

## Recommended Messaging

Onboarding

Welcome emails

Educational resources

Progress updates

Community invitations

Referral requests

---

## Avoid

Immediate upsells

Complex instructions

Neglect

---

## Success Metric

Customer satisfaction.

Retention.

Referrals.

Repeat purchases.

Consideration

# Transition Principles

Never attempt to force a customer into a later stage.

Each asset should focus on one transition only.

Examples

Attention → Interest

Interest → Consideration

Consideration → Evaluation

Evaluation → Decision

Decision → Success

Success → Advocacy

Attention

Interest

Consideration

Success

# Common Mistakes

Trying to close too early.

Repeating the same message at every stage.

Ignoring post-purchase communication.

Treating every visitor like a first-time visitor.

Changing brand voice between stages.

---

# Internal Journey Questions

Before generating copy ask:

What stage is the customer in?

What decision are they trying to make?

What uncertainty exists?

What emotion dominates?

What is the next psychological step?

---

# Ethical Guidance

Respect the customer's pace.

Guide.

Do not pressure.

Help them make an informed decision.

Trust built slowly lasts longer than decisions made under pressure.

---

# Final Rule

Do not optimize for immediate sales.

Optimize for moving the customer naturally to the next stage of their buying journey.
