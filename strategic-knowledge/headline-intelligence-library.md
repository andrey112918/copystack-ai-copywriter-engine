# Headline Intelligence Library

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

This library defines how CopyStack generates, evaluates and selects headlines.

A headline is not a title.

It is a decision-making mechanism.

Its purpose is to earn attention, create relevance and motivate the reader to continue.

Every headline should maximize the probability of reading the next sentence.

---

# Core Philosophy

The purpose of a headline is not to sell.

The purpose of a headline is to earn attention.

The body sells.

The headline earns permission.

# Benefit Headlines

## Objective

Communicate immediate value.

---

## Structure

Desired Outcome

*

Specific Benefit

Examples

Generate more qualified leads without increasing your ad spend.

Build a website that converts visitors into customers.

---

## Best For

Landing Pages

Sales Pages

Service Pages

---

# Problem Headlines

## Objective

Immediately identify with customer pain.

---

## Structure

Problem

↓

Consequence

Examples

Your website isn't losing traffic.

It's losing trust.

---

## Best For

Problem-aware audiences.

Meta Ads.

Landing Pages.

---

# Curiosity Headlines

## Objective

Create an information gap.

---

## Structure

Unexpected observation.

Hidden truth.

Contradiction.

Examples

The biggest reason your landing page isn't converting has nothing to do with design.

---

## Rule

Curiosity must always be resolved.

Never become clickbait.

---

# Detection Signals

Use Benefit headlines when:

Audience already wants the solution.

---

Use Problem headlines when:

Pain is obvious.

---

Use Curiosity headlines when:

Attention is low.

---

Use Proof headlines when:

Trust is weak.

---

Use Authority headlines when:

Expertise influences purchasing.

# Ethical Guidelines

Never use misleading headlines.

Never promise impossible outcomes.

Never sacrifice clarity for clicks.

A headline should attract the right reader—not everyone.

---

# Final Rule

The best headline is not the one that gets the most attention.

It is the one that attracts the right audience, creates the right expectation and naturally leads to the next sentence.
