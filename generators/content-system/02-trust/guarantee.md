# Guarantee Component

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

The Guarantee Component reduces perceived risk by clearly communicating how the business protects the customer if expectations are not met.

A guarantee should increase confidence—not create unrealistic expectations.

The objective is to make taking action feel safer.

---

# Core Philosophy

Customers rarely fear losing money.

They fear making the wrong decision.

A guarantee transfers part of that risk from the customer to the business.

Strong guarantees increase trust before they increase conversions.

---

# Component Objective

The Guarantee Component should:

Reduce perceived risk.

Increase confidence.

Clarify expectations.

Support premium positioning.

Strengthen the CTA.

---

# Guarantee Types

## Money-Back Guarantee

Return the customer's payment under clearly defined conditions.

Best For

Digital products.

Courses.

Software.

Subscriptions.

---

## Satisfaction Guarantee

Commit to improving the experience until agreed expectations are met.

Best For

Professional services.

Consulting.

Creative agencies.

---

## Revision Guarantee

Offer additional revisions if predefined quality standards are not achieved.

Best For

Design.

Copywriting.

Development.

Branding.

---

## Performance Guarantee

Promise measurable outcomes only when they are fully controllable.

Examples

Delivery time.

Response time.

System uptime.

Avoid guaranteeing business results outside your control.

---

## Process Guarantee

Guarantee transparency, communication and predictable execution.

Examples

Weekly updates.

Dedicated project manager.

Regular review meetings.

# Writing Principles

Explain:

What is guaranteed.

Who qualifies.

How it works.

Time limits.

Any conditions.

Use plain language.

Avoid legal jargon whenever possible.

# Ethical Guidelines

Never offer guarantees that cannot consistently be honored.

Never hide important conditions.

Never create guarantees purely for marketing purposes.

A guarantee should genuinely reduce customer risk.

---

# Final Rule

The strongest guarantee is not the biggest.

It is the one that customers genuinely believe will be honored.
