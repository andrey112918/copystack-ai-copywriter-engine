# Testimonials Component

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

The Testimonials Component strategically selects, structures and positions customer testimonials to increase credibility, reduce objections and strengthen trust.

Testimonials should never exist simply to fill space.

Every testimonial should reinforce a specific strategic objective.

---

# Core Philosophy

The most persuasive message is often spoken by a customer—not by the business.

Testimonials transfer trust.

Their purpose is to answer:

"Will this actually work for someone like me?"

---

# Component Objective

The Testimonials Component should:

Increase credibility.

Reduce perceived risk.

Support positioning.

Validate promises.

Reinforce emotional confidence.

Increase conversion probability.

# Placement Strategy

Testimonials should appear:

After value proposition.

↓

After benefits.

↓

Near pricing.

↓

Before CTA.

↓

Inside objection handling.

↓

Near guarantees.

Never place testimonials randomly.

---

# Testimonial Quality

Strong testimonials are:

Specific.

Authentic.

Measurable.

Relevant.

Natural.

Detailed.

Weak testimonials are:

Generic.

Anonymous.

Overly short.

Exaggerated.

Unspecific.

---

# Trust Elements

Whenever possible include:

Full name.

Company.

Photo.

Role.

Industry.

Website.

LinkedIn.

Verification.

The easier the testimonial is to verify,

the stronger it becomes.

# Ethical Guidelines

Never:

Invent testimonials.

Rewrite testimonials to change meaning.

Fabricate names.

Create fake companies.

Generate fictional reviews.

Remove important context.

Authenticity is always more persuasive than perfection.

---

# Final Rule

A testimonial should never answer:

"Was the customer happy?"

It should answer:

"Why should someone like me trust this business?"
