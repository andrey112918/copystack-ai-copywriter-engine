# Hero Section Component

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

The Hero Section is the most important content component in any landing page, homepage or sales page.

Its purpose is not to explain everything.

Its purpose is to immediately communicate:

* Relevance
* Value
* Differentiation
* Direction

The Hero Section determines whether the visitor continues reading.

---

# Core Philosophy

Visitors make an impression within seconds.

The Hero Section should answer:

What is this?

↓

Who is it for?

↓

Why should I care?

↓

What should I do next?

If any answer is unclear, the Hero Section has failed.

---

# Component Objective

The Hero Section should accomplish five goals.

Capture attention.

↓

Create relevance.

↓

Communicate value.

↓

Build curiosity or trust.

↓

Lead naturally toward the first CTA.

---

# Component Structure

Every Hero Section should contain:

Primary Headline

↓

Supporting Headline

↓

Value Proposition

↓

Primary CTA

↓

Optional Trust Signals

↓

Optional Supporting Visual

---

# Primary Headline

Purpose

Communicate the primary transformation.

Requirements

Clear.

Specific.

Benefit-driven.

Relevant.

Easy to scan.

Never clever at the expense of clarity.

---

# Supporting Headline

Purpose

Expand the promise.

Clarify:

Audience.

Problem.

Solution.

Outcome.

Support—not repeat—the headline.

---

# Value Proposition

Communicate:

What the offer is.

Who it helps.

What makes it different.

Why it matters.

Maximum clarity.

Minimum complexity.

---

# Primary CTA

Should represent the next logical step.

Examples

Book a Strategy Call

Request a Free Audit

Start Your Free Trial

Download the Guide

See How It Works

Never introduce multiple competing actions.

---

# Trust Signals

Optional depending on context.

Examples

Client logos

Review score

Years of experience

Projects completed

Certifications

Awards

Testimonials

Guarantees

Trust signals should support—not dominate—the Hero.

---

# Visual Strategy

Visuals should reinforce the message.

Examples

Founder photo

Product interface

Before / After

Customer result

Dashboard

Process illustration

Avoid decorative images without strategic value.

---

# Emotional Objective

The Hero should create one dominant emotion.

Examples

Confidence

Relief

Curiosity

Ambition

Security

Hope

Avoid mixing multiple emotional directions.

Increase Hero clarity when:

Cold traffic.

High bounce rate.

Complex offer.

Unknown brand.

Low awareness audience.

# Common Mistakes

Talking about the company first.

Using vague headlines.

Adding too many CTAs.

Explaining features before value.

Using stock images with no strategic purpose.

Creating curiosity without clarity.

Overloading the Hero with information.

# Ethical Guidelines

Never exaggerate outcomes.

Never fabricate trust signals.

Never use misleading visuals.

Never promise unrealistic results.

The Hero should create accurate expectations.

---

# Final Rule

The Hero Section earns the reader's attention.

Every other section earns the reader's trust.

If the Hero fails, the rest of the page may never be seen.
