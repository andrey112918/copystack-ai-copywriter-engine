# Pricing Section Component

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

The Pricing Section presents the commercial offer in a way that maximizes perceived value while minimizing price resistance.

Its objective is not to make the offer appear cheap.

Its objective is to make the investment feel justified.

Customers should understand what they receive before evaluating what they pay.

---

# Core Philosophy

People rarely buy the cheapest option.

They buy the option that appears to deliver the highest value for the perceived investment.

Price should never be introduced before value has been established.

---

# Component Objective

The Pricing Section should:

Present the investment.

Justify the price.

Reduce price objections.

Support premium positioning.

Lead naturally into the purchase decision.

---

Every pricing option should clearly state:

Deliverables.

Support.

Guarantees.

# Price Anchoring

When appropriate:

Present premium option first.

↓

Present recommended option.

↓

Present entry option.

Anchoring should feel natural.

Never manipulate.

# Comparison Table

Feature differences matter.

Enterprise options exist.

Avoid comparison tables for simple offers.

# Ethical Guidelines

Never hide fees.

Never create fake discounts.

Never fabricate urgency around pricing.

Never misrepresent savings.

Pricing should always be transparent, understandable and fair.

---

# Final Rule

Customers should never finish the Pricing Section asking:

"Why does it cost this much?"

They should finish asking:

"Why would I choose anything else?"
