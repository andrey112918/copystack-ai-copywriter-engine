# Scarcity Section Component

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

The Scarcity Section explains when availability is genuinely limited.

Its purpose is to increase the perceived importance of acting before an opportunity is no longer available.

Scarcity should communicate real limitations.

Never artificial ones.

---

# Core Philosophy

Scarcity is about availability.

Not pressure.

People naturally value resources that are genuinely limited.

Artificial scarcity destroys trust.

Real scarcity increases commitment.

---

# Component Objective

The Scarcity Section should:

Increase perceived value.

Explain genuine limitations.

Support premium positioning.

Encourage timely decisions.

Maintain long-term trust.

---

# Types of Scarcity

## Capacity Scarcity

Availability is limited because of delivery capacity.

Examples

Maximum number of active clients.

Limited consulting slots.

Limited monthly projects.

Best For

Agencies

Consultants

Freelancers

Professional Services

---

## Quantity Scarcity

Limited inventory.

Examples

Limited production run.

Limited product quantity.

Limited seats.

Limited licenses.

Best For

Products

Events

Courses

Software licenses

---

## Access Scarcity

Not everyone can access the offer.

Examples

Application required.

Invitation only.

Members only.

Private beta.

Best For

Premium brands

Communities

Enterprise products

---

## Expertise Scarcity

The resource is difficult to scale.

Examples

Founder involvement.

Senior consultant only.

Specialized expertise.

Manual implementation.

---

## Time Allocation Scarcity

Examples

Only two onboarding projects per month.

One strategy session per client.

Limited implementation windows.

# Writing Principles

Explain:

What is limited.

↓

Why it is limited.

↓

How this benefits the customer.

↓

What happens when availability is exhausted.

Never imply false limitations.

---

# Customer Benefit

Scarcity should benefit the customer.

Example

We limit the number of active projects to ensure every client receives direct strategic attention.

The limitation protects quality.

---

# Scarcity Communication

Strong scarcity explains:

Capacity.

Quality.

Support.

Personal attention.

Operational reality.

Weak scarcity simply says:

"Hurry."
