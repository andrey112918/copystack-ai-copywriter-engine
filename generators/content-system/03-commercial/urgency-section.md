# Urgency Section Component

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

The Urgency Section encourages timely action by explaining why delaying the decision may reduce value or postpone desired outcomes.

Its objective is not to pressure the customer.

Its objective is to help the customer understand the cost of waiting.

Urgency should accelerate decisions—not manipulate them.

---

# Core Philosophy

Urgency is about timing.

Not pressure.

The strongest urgency comes from reality.

Artificial urgency destroys trust.

Real urgency builds momentum.

---

# Component Objective

The Urgency Section should:

Encourage timely action.

Reduce procrastination.

Highlight opportunity cost.

Support the CTA.

Maintain trust.

---

# Types of Urgency

## Time-Based

Real deadlines.

Examples

Enrollment closes on a fixed date.

Price increase on a scheduled date.

Registration deadline.

Only use when verifiable.

---

## Opportunity Cost

Explain what happens by waiting.

Examples

Lost leads.

Delayed growth.

Missed revenue.

Longer recovery.

Often stronger than discounts.

---

## Seasonal

Examples

Tax season.

Holiday campaigns.

School enrollment.

Industry events.

Only use when relevant.

---

## Capacity-Based

Examples

Limited onboarding capacity.

Limited consulting availability.

Limited project slots.

Must reflect real operational constraints.

---

## Market-Based

Examples

Regulatory changes.

Technology shifts.

Market trends.

Competitive advantage.

Should educate, not create fear.

# Opportunity Cost

Focus on:

What the customer loses by waiting.

Examples

Every delayed launch postpones valuable customer feedback.

Do not exaggerate.

# Ethical Guidelines

Never create false deadlines.

Never invent limited availability.

# Final Rule

Urgency should answer one question:

"Why is acting today better than acting later?"

If there is no honest answer, the Urgency Section should not exist.
